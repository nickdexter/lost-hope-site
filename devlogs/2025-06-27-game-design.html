Game Design

Abandoned by your squad after an atomic nuke, fight through the streets of New York, outnumbered, and hunted by the remnants of the unlucky souls hit by the blast.

Potential Names

  • LOST HOPE
  • Here They Come
  • Left Behind

Overview

LOST HOPE is a horror wave-based survival FPS with an emphasis on satisfying gunplay.

Inspiration

Many different games have inspired the design of LOST HOPE, from visuals to mechanics.

Killing Floor (2009)

The gritty overrun look of Killing Floor has always been a favourite of mine. The atmosphere of the game makes you feel overpowered by the ZEDs (enemies) and it feels like you're truly fighting for your survival.

The game uses Unreal Engine 2.5 which achieves its lighting mainly through baked lightmaps and vertex lighting, with little real-time lights. This should be fairly easy to replicate in Unity.

Gunplay has always been Killing Floor's strong suit with satisfying sounds, animations, and overall gun feel. This is something I look to emulate in LOST HOPE.

Call of Duty: Zombies (2008)

COD: Zombies perks are purchased mid-game with points earned that game. They're non-permanent and must be unlocked each play session. I like the idea of adding a similar system to LOST HOPE.

PAYDAY 2 (2013)

PAYDAY's skill tree and builds are what kept me playing for over a thousand hours. Coming up with interesting builds combining different weapons, armour, perk decks, and skills to optimize for the highest difficulty.

LOST HOPE will feature skill trees as its main way of providing players with different play-styles.

Bulletstorm (2011)

Bulletstorm's skillshot system adds variety to what I'd otherwise consider rather bland gunplay. It encourages players to experiment with different weapons and discover combos for big point bonuses. Although rather arcadey, It's something I'm considering adding. It helps keep the gameplay feeling fresh.

PIGFACE (2025)

PIGFACE is another game I take visual inspiration from. I love the PS2 era look with its bilinear blurry textures and low-to-mid poly models.

Skill Tree

The skill tree will allow players to spend skill points to increase base stats, unlock new abilities, and buff different classes of weapons. Players are encouraged to experiment with different skill combinations to create unique builds accustomed to their play-style.

Skill points are unlocked by leveling up through regular gameplay.

Progression

Players can increase their level by earning EXP. For each level earned a skillpoint will be rewarded to spend on skills.

Players will gain EXP by:

  • Killing infected
    • Bonus for killing special infected
  • Surviving waves/rounds
    • Bonus multiplier for surviving final boss round
